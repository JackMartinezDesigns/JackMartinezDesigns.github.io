Africa x RI HandBags

The African Women’s Refugee Care Center (AWRC) on Broad Street in Providence supports refugee women through sewing-based entrepreneurship, using soft goods and textile practices as tools for self-sufficiency and cultural continuity.

I connected with AWRC while teaching soft goods design at the Rhode Island School of Design, initially seeking to introduce students to manufacturing as a site of social impact, which evolved into a deeper collaboration rooted in empowerment rather than extraction.

We paired bold lapa patterns with complementary solid fabrics to create visual balance while reducing material costs, making the bags more scalable and feasible for production.

The use of patterned textiles was carefully considered, including the design of reversible forms that allowed wearers to modulate visibility and helped mitigate the risk of the work being misinterpreted as cultural appropriation.

Centered on the seamstresses’ entrepreneurship goals, our collaborative process included developing detailed cost sheets, which supported informed pricing decisions and offered valuable lessons about valuing labor and sustaining higher wages.
