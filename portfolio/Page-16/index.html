Design Research



Design research is the foundation of my industrial design practice, guiding how I understand systems, stakeholders, and real-world constraints before proposing form. Through embedded observation, collaboration, and iterative testing, research ensures my design decisions are ethical, context-responsive, and built to last.


No. 001             Grocery Redesign

American diets are leaning more and more towards highly processed, carb-based foods. This research project investigates how the grocery store plays towards unhealthy eating, and designs a better grocery store for healthy eating.

No. 002             Gentrification Research

This project began as a way to understand what people thought of the term gentrification, beyond positive or negative, using maps and map-making kit users described what they understood of gentrification in their own city.

No. 003             Patchworks Research

Patchworks uses applied, community-embedded design research grounded in long-term partnerships with healthcare, harm reduction, housing, and food-access organizations. Through participatory observation, co-design, prototyping, and ethical reflection, the research generates repeatable tools that translate lived experience into durable, context-specific design interventions.

No. 004             Tap-Tap

What began as a design trends project turned into a display on the need for projects that help users develop empathy, asking how we can literally step into each others' shoes.
